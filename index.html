Getting Your Associates Degree in Nursing

Gaining an Associate Degree in Nursing takes at least two years of study. Going through the course gives you the skills and knowledge needed to succeed at helping every person who comes across your path. Learn the basics of how to properly care for patients of any age, how to deal with sicknesses and diseases, and how to help doctors find the right diagnosis for any patients.

Some of the courses every nurse must take can be found on this list. Click the drop down button to view. Note that this list does not include everything, just some of the basic courses.

  • ~Anatomy~
  • ~Physiology~
  • ~Health Concepts~
  • ~Psychology~
  • ~Health Care Concepts~
  • ~Microbiology~

Many nurses work in hospitals, helping doctors with sick patients, working to provide the best service they can for every patient in the hospital they can help. Some nurses choose instead to go into residential homes of elderly or disabled people. In residential homes, the nurses are often given more control over how the patients are cared for, and often have less patients than nurses working at a hospital. Others may even choose to go into the military as nurses, work in schools, or even just helping with research into pharmaceutics.
